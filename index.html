Your thesis idea is hiding in the field. Go find it.

Pakistan Policy Quest is a story-driven game that never asks “what is your research interest?” Instead you wander a cotton village in Muzaffargarh, a lane in Orangi, a courthouse under the banyans — and your choices quietly build a profile. Forty minutes later you leave with a complete, thesis-ready research proposal: question, real Pakistani datasets, a causal identification strategy, a 12-month plan, ethics, threats, extensions.

▶ Start playing Browse the Data Vault

Five acts, one proposal

Chai & Character. Pick a city desk and answer six sideways questions — which headline you read first, what bothered you last Eid. The engine listens between the lines.
Field Diaries. Three vignettes chosen adaptively from eight Pakistani field sites. What you choose to ask is the data.
The Data Bazaar. Real files — PSLM, PDHS, NSER, LFS, satellite layers — with years, variables, access routes, and honest limitations. Open two.
The Secretary's Question. A skeptical Additional Secretary attacks your correlation. Defend a real identification strategy — and survive the follow-up jab. (Kitchen-sink OLS is a trap. You will learn why.)
The Proposal Desk. Two or three tailored directions with pros and cons, then the full document — refine it, swap designs, export to Markdown or PDF, and grill an AI supervisor about it.

Vetted research designs

Across education, gender, health, poverty, climate, conflict, governance, and labor — each with equations, threats, real literature anchors, and feasibility ratings.

11

Real data sources

PSLM, HIES, LFS, PDHS, MICS, ASER, Census, BISP–NSER, household panels, admin records, satellite layers — with access routes and limitations written down honestly.

6

Method duels

DiD, staggered rollouts, RDD, IV, RCTs, and honest descriptive panels — each with its own assumptions, robustness plan, and a Secretary who checks whether you know them.

For instructors

Use it as a first-week assignment in research methods or development economics: each student plays once, exports their proposal, and defends it in class. The Data Vault doubles as a reference shelf for Pakistani microdata. All content lives in one readable file (js/data.js) — add your own designs, datasets, or vignettes.
